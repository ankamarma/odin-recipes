Pumpkin Soup

HOW TO MAKE PUMPKIN SOUP:

Instead of the usual autumn spices like cinnamon and nutmeg, this pureed pumpkin soup was inspired by Thai flavors. It's made especially creamy with the addition of coconut milk, while the Thai red curry paste and garlic lend depth and heat. Lemongrass, ginger, and a squeeze of fresh lime juice brighten the flavors in each spoonful. If you want a touch more heat, add extra sliced Fresno chiles as a garnish.

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 tablespoons canola oil
  • 1 small white onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised

STEPS

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over moderate heat. Add the onion and saut√© for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic, ginger, lemongrass, and curry paste and cook for 3 minutes. Add the pumpkin puree and broth, stirring to incorporate, then season with salt and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. Transfer the soup to a blender (or use an immersion blender in the pot) and puree until smooth. If needed, add additional water or broth to reach the desired consistency.
  4. Return the mixture to the saucepan. Serve warm and garnish with fresh cilantro and thinly sliced Fresno chiles.
